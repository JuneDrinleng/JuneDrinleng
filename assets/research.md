# Research

My research focuses on integrating deep learning with single-particle tracking to optimize the full workflow of particle trajectory analysis, especially trajectory extraction and downstream trajectory analytics. By combining data-driven models with quantitative biophysical analysis, I aim to improve the accuracy, robustness, and throughput of tracking in noisy and heterogeneous microscopy datasets. These advances enable us to decode the spatiotemporal dynamics of biophysical processes in complex micro/nano-scale systems from single-particle trajectories.

Research interests include:

• Develop deep learning-based single-particle tracking pipelines with a primary focus on robust trajectory linking and uncertainty-aware quality control;

• Build interpretable trajectory analysis frameworks to quantify diffusion modes, state transitions, confinement effects, and transient interactions in crowded micro/nano environments;

• Integrate physics-informed modeling with neural networks to improve the generalization of tracking algorithms across different imaging conditions and biological systems;

• Use large-scale trajectory datasets to reveal hidden spatiotemporal patterns of biomolecular transport and dynamic organization in complex biophysical processes.
